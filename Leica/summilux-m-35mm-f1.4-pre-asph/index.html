    Leica Summilux-M 35mm f/1.4 (Pre-ASPH)

    The Summilux-M 35mm f/1.4 pre-ASPH was produced from 1961 to 1994, when it was replaced by the current ASPH design. It is one of the most characterful wide-angle lenses in the M system — rendering with a distinctive veil and soft contrast wide open, transitioning to sharp, saturated results by f/2.8–f/4. The out-of-focus rendering, particularly the "cat's eye" bokeh from the oval aperture at wide stops, is considered part of its signature.

    Two main variants exist: the early steel rim (1961–1963), which has no filter thread and is rarer and more collectible, and the standard production version (1963–1994), which takes E46 filters. Service procedure is similar between variants.

    Tools & Materials

    • Spanner wrench (adjustable)
    • Rubber friction grip
    • JIS screwdrivers: #00, #0
    • 99% isopropyl alcohol
    • Lighter fluid (Ronsonol) — for grease removal
    • Lens tissue and Eclipse fluid
    • Cotton gloves
    • Soft cotton swabs
    • Helicoid grease (medium-light viscosity — Molykote EM-30L or similar)
    • Loupe (10x minimum)

    Before You Start

    Oil on aperture blades

    The most common service issue on pre-ASPH Summiluxes is oil migration onto the aperture blades from a degraded or over-greased helicoid. Inspect the blades before disassembling anything — hold the lens against a bright light and actuate the aperture ring. Oily blades appear shiny or move sluggishly.

    Know your variant

    The steel rim version has a different front bezel construction. The filter thread on standard versions is E46 — confirm before ordering a replacement part or adapter. Early steel rim examples may have slightly different aperture blade counts or internal arrangement; verify before ordering replacement parts.

    Notes

    • The pre-ASPH rendering wide open is intentional, not a flaw. Stopping down to f/2–f/2.8 cleans up contrast significantly while retaining the warm, three-dimensional character.
    • Infinity focus can shift after helicoid regrease. Verify on a distant subject and adjust the focus tab position if needed.
    • The ASPH version (1994–present) is a completely different optical design and is not interchangeable at the element level.

    Service Steps

    The lens overlal was in good condition. The optical block just uscrews from the helicoid assembly, but in my copy the optical block assembly itself was loose because of a set screw that wasn't fully tightened. I managed to unscrew it, loosen the set screw in the optical assembly, clean up the aluminum shavings, then re-assemble the optical block and tighten the set screw. Not fun but it had to be done.

    The helicoid assemlby was somewhat stiff, so the first task is figuring out how to get inside it.
